About Fall Armyworm

Understanding Fall Armyworm

Fig 1: Maize leaf with Fall Armyworm

The Fall Armyworm (FAW) (Spodoptera frugiperda) is a highly destructive pest that primarily attacks maize and cereal crops worldwide. Originally from the Americas, FAW has rapidly spread to Africa, including Uganda, due to favorable climatic conditions and global trade.

It causes severe crop losses, reduced yields, and financial instability, especially for small-scale farmers with limited pest control resources.

Early detection is crucial, but most farmers rely on ineffective visual inspections, leading to delayed responses. Current methods like chemical pesticides are costly and pose environmental and health risks when misused. This study aims to collect data on FAW prevalence, its impact, and existing management strategies among smallholder farmers.

The Challenge

Problem statement

Fig 2: Early stages of Fall Armyworm on a maize leaf

Fall Armyworm (Spodoptera frugiperda) is a severe pest affecting maize farmers in Uganda. Farmers struggle to identify infestations early due to a lack of affordable tools and resources. Instead, they rely on traditional practices and personal observations, which are often inaccurate and delay intervention.

These delays allow the pest to cause extensive damage, reducing yields and increasing the economic burden on smallholder farmers. Access to reliable pest management strategies, including diagnostic tools and expert guidance, is limited in rural areas, further worsening the problem.

While technological solutions such as mobile apps could bridge this gap, farmers' perceptions of and readiness for adopting these tools remain unclear. This study aims to address these challenges by collecting data to understand farmers' experiences, practices, and openness to technology-driven solutions.

Our Goal

Project Objectives

Fig 3: Maize leaf with severe fall armyworm effect

Assess the awareness and challenges faced by maize farmers in identifying and managing Fall Armyworm (FAW) infestations.

Explore farmers' perceptions of using technology as a solution, including their experiences and knowledge of FAW's impact.

Evaluate readiness to adopt digital tools for early detection and management of FAW infestations.
