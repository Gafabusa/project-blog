My Contributions

Project Ideation

From the beginning, I actively encouraged my team to focus on a project related to agriculture, recognizing the critical role technology can play in improving farming practices. I strongly believed that leveraging software solutions for agriculture could have a meaningful impact on food security and farmers' livelihoods. Through discussions and brainstorming sessions, we explored various agricultural challenges, and I played a key role in steering our team toward addressing a real-world problem. As we refined our ideas, we had the opportunity to engage with experts, and under the guidance of the Head of NARO (National Agricultural Research Organization), we refined our focus to develop a Fall Armyworm (FAW) Diagnosis System. This shift allowed us to create a practical, technology-driven solution that directly addresses a major challenge faced by farmers in Uganda, and I am proud to have been an integral part of this idea formulation process.

Field Data Collection

Beyond contributing to the project's idea formulation, I also actively participated in the data collection process. As a team, we gathered crucial data from NARO (National Agricultural Research Organization) to ensure our solution was grounded in real agricultural challenges and expert insights. In addition to obtaining information from NARO, I personally joined my team members in the field, visiting farms and interacting with local farmers to collect firsthand data on Fall Armyworm infestations and their impact. Engaging directly with farmers gave me a deeper understanding of the challenges they face and further reinforced the importance of developing a practical, technology-driven solution for early pest detection and management.

Community Section Development

I am also going to work on the Community Section of our application, which will serve as an interactive space for farmers to share experiences, ask questions, and receive expert advice on FAW management. This feature will be designed to foster collaboration and knowledge-sharing among farmers, researchers, and agricultural experts. By creating a user-friendly and engaging platform, I aim to bridge the information gap and empower farmers with real-time support and practical solutions for dealing with Fall Armyworm infestations. This section will also include discussion threads, expert insights, and success stories to encourage widespread participation and collective problem-solving.

FAW Distribution Map

As part of my contributions to this project, I will also be responsible for developing the FAW Distribution Map in our application. This feature will visually represent the spread of Fall Armyworm infestations across different districts in Uganda, helping farmers, researchers, and policymakers track affected areas and make informed decisions. By integrating geospatial data and mapping technologies, I aim to create an intuitive and user-friendly interface that will allow users to view, analyze, and monitor FAW outbreaks in real time. This will be a crucial tool in improving pest management strategies and resource allocation for affected farmers.

Model Training

In addition to my contributions to the Community Section and the FAW Distribution Map, I am also actively involved in training of the machine learning model for FAW diagnosis. My role will include preparing and cleaning the dataset, selecting suitable algorithms, and fine-tuning the model to ensure high accuracy in detecting FAW infestations. By leveraging my expertise in data analysis and machine learning, I aim to develop a robust and reliable diagnostic tool that can assist farmers in early pest detection and management. This model will be a key component of our application, providing users with accurate and timely information to protect their crops and improve agricultural productivity.
